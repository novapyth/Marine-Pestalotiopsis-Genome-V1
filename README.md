### *Pestalotiopsis* sp. KF079

Marine fungi constitute an underexplored reservoir of genetic and metabolic diversity capable of producing structurally unique secondary metabolites. Nevertheless, genomic resources describing their biosynthetic capabilities remain scarce. To expand our understanding of marine fungal biosynthesis, we sequenced and characterised the genome of *Pestalotiopsis* sp. KF079, a marine fungal isolate collected from the German Wadden Sea.

Whole-genome sequencing and assembly produced a genome of approximately 47.5 Mb. Comprehensive genome annotation identified 67 biosynthetic gene clusters potentially involved in the synthesis of diverse secondary metabolites. Comparative analyses demonstrated that approximately 97% of these clusters are novel, exhibiting limited similarity to known biosynthetic pathways reported in public databases. This exceptionally high level of novelty indicates that *Pestalotiopsis* sp. KF079 possesses substantial untapped biosynthetic potential.

To investigate the transcriptional activity of these gene clusters, RNA-sequencing analysis was conducted under laboratory growth conditions. Similar to observations in other filamentous fungi, only a small fraction of the predicted BGCs was actively expressed, whereas most clusters remained silent. The presence of numerous inactive pathways suggests that this fungus may produce a much broader spectrum of metabolites under alternative environmental or physiological conditions.

Beyond biosynthetic pathway discovery, genome analysis revealed important features related to the organism’s biology, adaptation to marine habitats, and ecological lifestyle. The large number of unique biosynthetic gene clusters uncovered in *Pestalotiopsis* sp. KF079 provides a rich foundation for future studies aimed at identifying novel natural products and bioactive compounds. These genomic resources will facilitate future genetic, functional, and comparative investigations while supporting the exploration of marine fungi as sources of new therapeutic and industrial molecules.
